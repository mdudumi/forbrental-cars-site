Explore Albania: Cars, Driving & Culture

Your go-to source for detailed guides on renting cars, navigating Albanian roads safely, and uncovering the rich history and unique curiosities of Albania—from Tirana’s bustling streets to the ancient ruins of Butrint.

Renting Cars in Albania

Whether you’re arriving at Tirana International Airport or picking up your vehicle in the city center, understanding local rental procedures will ensure a smooth start to your journey.

Most agencies require a valid driver’s license (held for at least one year), a passport or national ID, and a credit card for the security deposit. Insurance packages range from basic third-party coverage to comprehensive collision damage waivers—choose according to your comfort level and itinerary.

  • Book early during summer (June–August) to secure the best rates.
  • Opt for a full-to-full fuel policy—it’s the most transparent option.
  • Inspect the vehicle carefully for any scratches or dents before signing.

Driving in Albania

Albania offers a diverse driving experience: coastal highways, mountain passes, and urban traffic. Familiarize yourself with the rules and best practices to stay safe.

  • Road Conditions: Major routes are paved, but rural roads may be uneven—reduce speed on backcountry stretches.
  • Signage: Most follow European norms, though local directional markers appear in Albanian.
  • Speed Limits: 50 km/h in cities, 80 km/h on open roads, 110 km/h on highways—speed cameras are common near towns.
  • Mountain Safety: Always carry water, blankets, and a physical map; mobile coverage can be spotty.

Discover Albania: History & Curiosities

Albania blends millennia of history with warm hospitality. From UNESCO World Heritage sites to modern urban hubs, there is so much to explore.

Ancient Roots: Visit Butrint, an archaeological site founded by the Greeks in the 8th century BC, then expanded by Romans and Byzantines.

Cultural Gems: Experience a live iso-polyphonic concert in the Gjirokastër Old Town or browse Skanderbeg Square’s lively cafes in Tirana.

Local Curiosities: Albanians have a custom called Besa—an oath of honor—reflecting deep-rooted traditions of hospitality and trust.
